# Pillar Rotation Logic

## Default pillar set (adjust to the user's actual business)
1. Product/education — how the product works, tips, use cases
2. Customer stories — case studies, testimonials, results
3. Industry commentary — trends, opinions, contrarian takes
4. Behind-the-scenes — team, process, culture
5. Personal brand / founder voice — lessons, opinions, story

## Rotation principles
- No pillar should repeat on consecutive scheduled days for the same platform.
- Over a week, aim for the pillar with the strongest business impact (usually product/education or customer stories) to get 40-50% of slots, with the rest distributed across the others.
- Reserve behind-the-scenes and personal-brand pillars for platforms/formats where they land best (Instagram, TikTok, personal LinkedIn) rather than every platform equally.
- If there's a fixed-date item (launch, event), it takes priority in the slot closest to the actual date, and the pillar rotation resumes around it — don't force an unrelated pillar into that slot just to keep rotation "clean."

## Weekly rotation example (5 posts/week, single platform)
| Day | Pillar |
|---|---|
| Mon | Industry commentary |
| Wed | Product/education |
| Thu | Customer story |
| Fri | Behind-the-scenes or personal brand (alternate weekly) |
| Sun/weekend (if applicable) | Product/education or customer story, whichever wasn't used earlier that week |

Adjust days to the user's actual posting rhythm — this is a rotation logic example, not a fixed schedule.

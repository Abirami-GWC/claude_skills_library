# Platform Content Norms

Use this to calibrate tone, length, and format before generating ideas.

## LinkedIn
- Tone: professional but personal; first-person stories and lessons outperform corporate announcements.
- Length: 150-300 words performs best; first 2-3 lines must work as a standalone hook (feed truncates after ~210 characters).
- Formats that work: text-only stories, carousels (PDF) for frameworks/lists, single strong image + caption.
- Avoid: heavy hashtag stacks (2-3 max), external links in the main post (add in first comment instead).

## Instagram
- Tone: visual-first, casual, aspirational or relatable.
- Length: caption can be longer (educational carousels reward depth) but hook line must land in first 1-2 lines before "more" truncation.
- Formats that work: carousels (educational/listicle), Reels (hook in first 1.5 seconds), single image with strong caption.
- Hashtags: 3-8 relevant ones, not 30.

## X / Twitter
- Tone: punchy, opinionated, conversational. Rewards hot takes and specificity.
- Length: single tweets should be one idea, one sentence structure. Threads: first tweet is 90% of the work — it must stand alone as a complete thought or question.
- Formats that work: single-line observations, threads (numbered or not), quote-style insight.
- Avoid: burying the point in tweet 3 of a thread — the hook carries the whole thing.

## TikTok
- Tone: energetic, native, unpolished-feeling (over-produced reads as an ad).
- Length: hook must land in first 1-2 seconds of spoken/on-screen text. 15-45 sec sweet spot for informational content.
- Formats that work: talking-head with on-screen text hook, POV/story format, before-after, trend-audio adaptation.

## Facebook
- Tone: warmer, more community/group-oriented than LinkedIn; works well for local business, community, and older demographics.
- Length: similar to LinkedIn but slightly more casual; native video and photo albums outperform links.

## General principles across platforms
- The first line is doing 80% of the work everywhere. If the hook doesn't create curiosity, tension, or clear value, nothing after it matters.
- Native format beats polished format — a slightly rough, real photo or video usually outperforms stock-feeling content.
- One idea per post. Don't try to make one post do three jobs.

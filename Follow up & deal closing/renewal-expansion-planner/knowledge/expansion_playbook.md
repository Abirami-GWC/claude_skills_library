# Expansion / Upsell Playbook

Which upsell path fits which situation. Use this alongside the usage-signal framing in `mappings/signal_to_framing.yaml` — this file is about *what* to offer, the mapping file is about *when and how* to bring it up.

## More seats/licenses

Fits when: usage is growing within existing departments, or new departments have organically started using the product informally.
Pitch angle: frame as "formalizing what's already happening" rather than a cold upsell.

## New module/add-on (e.g. predictive analytics, advanced reporting)

Fits when: the champion or end users have asked about a capability the current package doesn't include, or a stated business goal (e.g. "we want to get ahead of issues, not just report on them after the fact") maps to the add-on's value.
Pitch angle: tie directly to the specific goal or question that was raised — never a generic "you might also like."

## Tier upgrade (e.g. moving from Core to a higher support/SLA tier)

Fits when: the account's usage or business criticality has grown enough that current support/SLA levels feel thin (e.g. more urgent tickets, more business-critical use cases).
Pitch angle: frame around risk reduction and support responsiveness, not just "more features."

## Multi-year commitment (renewal-timing lever, not a product upsell)

Fits when: usage is growing or stable and the account is a good long-term fit — offering a multi-year term at a discount can be pitched as a way to lock in savings rather than an upsell per se.

## What not to do

Never pitch more than one expansion path in the same renewal conversation unless the account has explicitly asked about multiple things — one clear, well-targeted offer converts better than a menu.

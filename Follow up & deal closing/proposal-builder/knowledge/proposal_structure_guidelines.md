# Proposal Structure Guidelines

## Cover / intro

One paragraph. State the client's name, the date, and a one-sentence summary of what's being proposed and why — tie it explicitly to something the client said they need, not a generic capability statement.

## Scope of work

Break into clear sub-sections if there are multiple products/services. Each item gets a one-line description of what it does and, ideally, what problem it solves for this specific client.

## Pricing table

Always show: item, unit price, quantity/term, any discount applied, and the final total. Never show only a final number without the underlying math — clients and their finance teams want to see how the number was derived. Include the proposal's validity period near the total, not buried in the terms section.

## Terms & next steps

Payment terms, delivery/onboarding timeline, and a single clear call to action (what happens immediately after signature). Avoid vague "let's discuss" closings — state the concrete next step.

## Signature block

Standard two-party signature lines (name, title, date) for both the client and the vendor.

## Tone

Confident and consultative, not salesy. Write as though addressing someone who has already agreed there's a fit and just needs the specifics confirmed.

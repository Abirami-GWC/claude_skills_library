# Email Writing Guidelines

## Length

- Nudge, contract chase, re-engagement: 3-6 sentences. If it runs longer, cut detail rather than losing a required section (subject, greeting, one concrete ask, close).
- Recap: may run to 8-10 sentences / a short bullet list, since it needs to cover discussion points accurately — but stay under ~250 words total.

## Tone

- Professional and warm. Never overly salesy, never pushy, never guilt-tripping about silence.
- Direct — state the one thing you want the recipient to do. Don't bury the ask.
- No exclamation points stacked for enthusiasm; no "just circling back!" filler openers.

## Structure

Subject line → greeting → one line of context/reason for writing → the specific ask or recap content → close with a clear next step → sign-off.

## Signature

Default sign-off: "[Your name]" placeholder, unless the user has a saved signature block. Do not invent a title, phone number, or company tagline that wasn't given.

## What never belongs in these emails

- A fabricated deadline, discount, or executive-level commitment.
- Overpromising a timeline that wasn't confirmed internally.
- Disparaging a named competitor.
- Legal/contractual language beyond what was explicitly provided — that belongs in the actual contract, not the email.

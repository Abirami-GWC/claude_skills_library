# Email Copywriting Frameworks

## Purpose
Structural frameworks for writing the body copy of an individual email once its strategic role in the sequence is known.

## Introduction
Once you know what job an email needs to do (educate, build trust, handle an objection, close), these frameworks provide the internal structure for that email's body copy.

## Detailed Explanation

### AIDA (Attention, Interest, Desire, Action)
Best for: promotional and launch emails.
- Attention: a hook line that stops the scroll.
- Interest: why this matters to them specifically.
- Desire: paint the outcome/benefit vividly.
- Action: one clear CTA.

### PAS (Problem, Agitate, Solve)
Best for: nurture and win-back emails.
- Problem: name a specific pain point.
- Agitate: make the cost of not solving it concrete.
- Solve: present the solution (content or product) as the resolution.

### StoryBrand-style (Character, Problem, Guide, Plan, Call to Action)
Best for: welcome and brand-story emails.
- Position the reader as the hero facing a problem.
- Position the sender as the guide with empathy + authority.
- Give a simple plan (3 steps or fewer).
- End with a clear call to action.

### Value-First / Educational
Best for: mid-nurture, onboarding.
- Deliver one specific, usable insight or tip up front.
- No pitch, or a very soft one-line mention at the end.
- Builds trust equity to be spent later in the sequence.

## Professional Guidance
Match the framework to the email's job, not the sequence type as a whole — a nurture sequence might use PAS in email 2 and StoryBrand in email 1.

## Examples
A win-back email using PAS: name the specific value the customer is missing (Problem), show what continuing to miss it costs them (Agitate), then offer a simple way back in (Solve).

## Common Mistakes
- Using AIDA for every email regardless of its job, making the sequence feel repetitive
- Skipping the "agitate" step and jumping straight to the pitch, which reads as pushy
- Overloading a single email with more than one framework at once

## Best Practices
- Pick one framework per email and follow it cleanly rather than mixing several.
- Keep body copy scannable: short paragraphs (1-3 sentences), occasional bolding of key phrases, no walls of text.
- End every email with exactly one CTA, restated at most once (e.g., in the body and again as a P.S.).

## Summary
Choose the internal structure (AIDA, PAS, StoryBrand, value-first) based on what job the individual email needs to do within the larger sequence arc.

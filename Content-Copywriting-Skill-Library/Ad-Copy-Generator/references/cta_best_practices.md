# CTA Best Practices

## Purpose

Provides a framework for selecting and writing calls-to-action that match funnel stage, channel convention, and offer type.

## Introduction

The CTA is where intent converts to action. A CTA mismatched to funnel stage (e.g., "Buy Now" shown to someone who has never heard of the product) creates friction and suppresses conversion, even when the rest of the ad is strong.

## Detailed Explanation

### CTA by Funnel Stage

| Funnel stage | Appropriate CTA examples |
|---|---|
| Cold / awareness | "Learn more," "See how it works," "Discover" |
| Consideration | "Get started free," "See pricing," "Try it free," "Compare plans" |
| Decision / bottom funnel | "Buy now," "Start your trial," "Claim your spot," "Get X% off" |
| Retargeting | "Come back and save X%," "Still thinking it over? Here's Y," "Finish your order" |

### Low-Friction vs. High-Commitment CTAs

- Low-friction ("Learn more," "See how") suits cold traffic and reduces bounce.
- High-commitment ("Buy now," "Sign up") suits warm/bottom-funnel traffic that already has intent.
- Mismatching these (high-commitment CTA on cold traffic) typically increases cost-per-click without improving conversion quality.

### CTA Writing Principles

1. **Lead with a verb.** "Start," "Get," "See," "Claim," "Try" — action-oriented, not passive ("Learn More About Our Product" → "Learn more").
2. **Be specific when possible.** "Get your free 14-day trial" beats "Sign up."
3. **Reduce perceived risk when appropriate.** "Try free, no credit card" lowers friction for hesitant audiences.
4. **Match urgency to reality.** Only use time-based urgency ("Ends Friday," "3 spots left") if genuinely true — fabricated urgency is a manipulative dark pattern and erodes trust when discovered.
5. **First person occasionally outperforms second person** in some channels/tests ("Start my free trial" vs. "Start your free trial") — worth offering as a variation when appropriate.

## Professional Guidance

- Never invent scarcity or urgency (fake countdowns, fake low-stock claims) — if the user wants urgency-based CTAs, confirm the urgency is real first.
- Match CTA commitment level to the ad's targeting/funnel context whenever known.
- Keep CTAs short — most channels truncate or de-prioritize long CTA text.

## Examples

**Cold traffic, SaaS product:** "See how it works" (low friction, curiosity-preserving)
**Bottom-funnel retargeting, same product:** "Finish setting up your account — takes 2 minutes"

## Common Mistakes

- Using "Buy Now" on top-of-funnel, cold-audience ads.
- Vague CTAs ("Click here," "Learn more about us") with no specificity where specificity is possible.
- Manufactured urgency/scarcity not based in fact.
- CTA copy that doesn't match what actually happens after the click (e.g., "Buy now" leading to a lead-gen form, not a purchase).

## Best Practices

- Always state the actual next step accurately (don't say "Buy now" if it leads to a quote request).
- Offer at least one low-friction and one higher-commitment CTA variation when funnel stage is ambiguous, and let the user pick based on their actual targeting.

## Summary

CTAs should lead with a verb, match funnel stage and real commitment level, stay specific where possible, and never rely on fabricated urgency or scarcity.

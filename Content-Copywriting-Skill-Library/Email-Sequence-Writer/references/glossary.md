# Glossary

## Purpose
Shared terminology for email marketing so outputs use consistent, correct terms.

- **ESP**: Email Service Provider (e.g., Klaviyo, Mailchimp, HubSpot) — the platform that sends the emails.
- **Open rate**: % of delivered emails that were opened.
- **Click-through rate (CTR)**: % of delivered (or opened) emails resulting in a click.
- **Cadence**: The timing/frequency pattern of a sequence.
- **Drip campaign**: A sequence sent on a fixed schedule regardless of behavior.
- **Trigger-based sequence**: A sequence where each email fires based on a user action/milestone rather than a fixed calendar day.
- **Segmentation**: Dividing an audience into groups to send more relevant content.
- **Preview text (preheader)**: The snippet shown after the subject line in most inboxes.
- **List hygiene**: Practices to keep an email list engaged and remove non-responders (protects deliverability).
- **Win-back / re-engagement**: A sequence aimed at reactivating dormant subscribers or customers.
- **Lead magnet**: A free resource offered in exchange for an email address.

## Summary
Consistent terminology keeps sequence outputs interoperable with any ESP and easy for marketing teams to hand off.

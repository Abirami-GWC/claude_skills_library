# CTA Best Practices (Landing Page Context)

## Purpose
Guidance specific to writing and placing calls to action on landing pages, where CTA design differs from email or ad contexts.

## Introduction
A landing page typically has one true conversion goal; every CTA on the page — however many times it appears — should point to that same action in the same words.

## Detailed Explanation
- **One primary CTA phrase, repeated.** Use the exact same CTA wording in the hero, mid-page, and closing section (e.g., always "Start my free trial," never alternating with "Sign up now" and "Get started").
- **Specific over generic.** "Start my free trial" beats "Submit" or "Learn more" when the actual next step is a trial signup.
- **Reduce perceived friction near the CTA.** Add micro-copy beneath the button addressing the most common hesitation: "No credit card required," "Cancel anytime," "Takes 2 minutes."
- **Place a CTA at every natural pause point**: end of hero, after benefits, after social proof, and at the final closing section — not only once at the bottom.
- **Secondary CTAs, if present, should be clearly lower-commitment** (e.g., "Watch a 2-minute demo" alongside "Start my free trial") and visually/textually secondary.

## Examples
- Weak: hero says "Get Started," mid-page says "Try It Now," footer says "Sign Up Today" (three different phrases, adds friction/confusion)
- Strong: all three instances say "Start my free trial" with consistent micro-copy underneath

## Common Mistakes
- Alternating CTA wording throughout the page, which increases cognitive load
- Multiple competing primary CTAs (e.g., "Buy Now" and "Book a Demo" given equal visual/textual weight)
- No friction-reducing micro-copy near high-commitment CTAs

## Best Practices
- Decide the single primary conversion action before writing any copy, then lock the CTA phrase early.
- Use micro-copy to address the single most common hesitation for that specific offer.
- If a secondary CTA exists, make its lower commitment explicit in the wording itself.

## Summary
One consistent, specific CTA phrase repeated at every natural pause point, supported by friction-reducing micro-copy.

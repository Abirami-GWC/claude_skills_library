# Top objections playbook

Pre-built responses to the objections a team hears most weeks. General-purpose — use this when
`scripts/classify_objection.py` (or reasoning) returns `category: playbook`.

## How to use this file

Match the incoming objection to the closest row below by meaning, not exact wording — prospects
never phrase it exactly like the trigger phrase. Use the response frame as a direction to write
in, not a script to read verbatim. If nothing matches closely, say so and offer to draft a new
entry rather than forcing a weak match.

## The 10 objections

| # | Objection (as prospect says it) | Response frame | Rationale |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | "We already have a security vendor" | Acknowledge, then ask what's prompting them to take this call at all — usually reveals a gap. Position as complementary/upgrade, not rip-and-replace. | Switching costs are the real objection underneath; don't argue against loyalty, surface the unmet need. |
| 2 | "Now isn't a good time" | Ask what timeline would work and what's driving it (budget cycle, project load). Offer to check back rather than push. | Distinguishes a real timing constraint from a soft no. |
| 3 | "We need to think about it" | Ask directly what's the one thing they're unsure about. | Vague stalls usually hide one specific concern — surface it. |
| 4 | "I need to run this by [stakeholder]" | Offer to join that conversation or provide a one-pager for them to forward. | Removes friction from internal championing. |
| 5 | "This seems complex to implement" | Walk through onboarding timeline and what's handled for them vs. what they own. | Complexity fear is usually about their own bandwidth, not the product. |
| 6 | "We tried something like this before and it didn't work" | Ask what specifically failed — tooling, support, adoption — before responding. | Don't defend against a failure mode that may not apply here. |
| 7 | "We're happy with what we have" | Ask what "happy" covers — have they had an incident, an audit finding, a near-miss recently. | Satisfaction often means "no fire yet," not "no gap." |
| 8 | "Send me some information and I'll get back to you" | Send it, but ask one narrowing question first so the follow-up isn't cold. | Prevents the info request from being a dead end. |
| 9 | "We're a small team, we don't need enterprise tooling" | Reframe around what breaks at their current scale, not enterprise features they don't need. | Avoid overselling; match the pitch to their actual size. |
| 10 | "Compliance/procurement will slow this down" | Ask what's usually required and offer to pre-package what procurement needs. | Turns a blocker into a next step you can help with. |

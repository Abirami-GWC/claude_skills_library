# Email Writing Guidelines (company communication standards)

## Writing Rules

- Professional, warm tone — never overly salesy or pushy.
- Keep the email under 300 words.
- Use bullet points for discussion topics, questions, and documents — not
  long paragraphs.
- Always mention the next meeting if one was scheduled.
- Always include action items (documents to send, follow-ups needed).
- Always thank the customer for their time.
- Always end with a consistent company signature block:
  ```
  Best regards,
  [Sales Representative Name]
  WatchGuard Technologies
  ```

## Tone guidance

- Address the primary contact by first name if known; use "team" or "there"
  if no name is available — never guess a name.
- Avoid overpromising: don't state pricing, discounts, or firm commitments
  that weren't explicitly discussed and agreed to in the meeting.
- If a customer question wasn't answered live, say so honestly ("we'll follow
  up with details on this shortly") rather than fabricating an answer.

## Subject line conventions

Use a short, warm subject line, e.g.:
- "Thank You for Your Time Today"
- "Thank You for Today's Meeting"
- "Following Up on Our Demo"

Avoid generic/salesy subject lines like "Exciting Next Steps!!" or anything
with excessive punctuation or urgency framing.

## Length and structure priority

If the notes contain more content than fits comfortably under 300 words,
trim detail within each bullet rather than dropping a required section
(Meeting Summary, Documents, Next Meeting, Closing must always appear if the
underlying information exists in the notes).

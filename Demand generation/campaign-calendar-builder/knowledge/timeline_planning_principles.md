# Timeline Planning Principles

## Typical minimum lead times by activity type

| Activity | Typical minimum lead time | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Organic social post | Same day–1 day | Low production overhead |
| Email send | 2–3 days | Copy review, list segmentation, QA send |
| Blog post | 3–5 days | Drafting, editing, SEO pass |
| Paid social/ads creative | 5–10 days | Creative production, targeting setup, approval |
| Webinar | 2–4 weeks | Speaker coordination, registration page, promotion runway |
| Press/PR outreach | 2–4 weeks | Journalist lead time, embargo coordination |
| Video production | 1–3 weeks depending on complexity | Scripting, filming, editing |
| Partner/co-marketing content | 2–4 weeks | Cross-org coordination and approval |

These are typical norms, not fixed rules — an organization's actual lead
times may differ. If the user has stated different constraints, use theirs;
otherwise use this table as a reasonable default and flag it as such.

## Common dependency patterns

- **Asset before promotion**: any post/email promoting a piece of content
  depends on that content being published/live first (or at minimum,
  scheduled to go live at or before the promotion's send time).
- **Approval before send**: paid creative, PR pitches, and press releases
  typically depend on an internal approval step — factor this into the lead time.
- **Registration before reminder**: webinar reminder emails depend on the
  registration page being live and registrations having started.
- **Announcement before follow-up**: a nurture sequence depends on the
  initial announcement having sent first.

## Sequencing principle: work backward from the anchor date

Given a launch/anchor date, work backward: identify the activity with the
longest lead time first (often PR or a webinar), and schedule its
prerequisite steps far enough before the anchor date to be ready. Then layer
in shorter-lead-time activities (social, email) closer to or on the anchor date itself.

## Buffer time

Add a buffer (typically 1–2 days minimum, more for high-stakes activities)
between a dependency's completion and the dependent activity's scheduled
date — scheduling back-to-back with zero buffer means any slip cascades
immediately into a missed date.

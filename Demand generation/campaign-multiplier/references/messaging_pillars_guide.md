# Deriving Messaging Pillars from One Asset

A messaging pillar is not a channel, a format, or a feature — it's a single
claim or theme the whole campaign is built to prove.

## How to extract pillars from a source asset

1. **Read for the core claim.** What is the one sentence this asset is
   ultimately arguing or demonstrating? That's pillar candidate #1.
2. **Look for supporting angles that could stand alone.** A product launch
   post might contain a technical angle (how it works), an outcome angle
   (what changes for the user), and a proof angle (early results/customer
   reaction). Each of these can be its own pillar if strong enough to carry a
   full channel's worth of content.
3. **Cap at three.** More than three pillars stops being a focused campaign
   and starts being a content dump. If more than three strong angles exist,
   pick the three most relevant to the stated audience/goal and hold the rest
   for a future campaign.

## Good vs. weak pillars

Weak (feature-level, not a narrative): "We added a new dashboard."
Good (outcome-level, carries a story): "Teams get visibility into their data
in real time instead of waiting on weekly reports."

Weak (too broad to differentiate any channel content): "We're innovative."
Good (specific enough to generate distinct angles per channel): "This release
removes the single biggest reason customers said they almost churned last quarter."

## Checking pillar-to-channel fit

Once pillars are set, each channel in the brief should pick up one pillar as
its lead angle (a channel can nod to a second pillar, but shouldn't try to
carry all three at once — that's how channel content ends up vague). If a
channel's angle doesn't clearly map to any pillar, either the pillar set is
incomplete or that channel doesn't belong in this campaign.

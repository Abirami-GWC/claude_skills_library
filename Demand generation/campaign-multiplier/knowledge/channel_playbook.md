# Channel Playbook

Characteristics and best-fit use cases for common campaign channels. Use this
to select channels that actually serve the stated goal — not every campaign
needs every channel.

## Email (owned list)
- **Best for**: nurturing an existing audience, driving a specific action (signup, download, purchase)
- **Strength**: highest-intent audience, direct CTA, measurable
- **Weakness**: limited to people already on the list — not a reach/awareness channel
- **Typical metric**: open rate, click-through rate, conversion rate

## Blog / owned content
- **Best for**: SEO-driven discovery, establishing depth/authority, giving
  other channels something to link back to
- **Strength**: compounding long-term traffic, canonical home for the full narrative
- **Weakness**: slow to drive immediate action; needs distribution to be seen at all
- **Typical metric**: organic traffic, time on page, downstream conversions

## LinkedIn (organic)
- **Best for**: B2B awareness, thought leadership, reaching a professional/decision-maker audience
- **Strength**: strong for B2B credibility-building
- **Weakness**: organic reach is inconsistent; works best with a consistent
  posting cadence, not one-off posts
- **Typical metric**: engagement rate, follower growth, click-through to owned content

## X / Twitter (organic)
- **Best for**: real-time announcements, developer/technical audiences, fast-moving conversation
- **Strength**: speed, direct engagement with a community
- **Weakness**: short shelf life per post; needs volume/frequency to sustain visibility
- **Typical metric**: engagement rate, link clicks

## Paid social (LinkedIn/Meta/X ads)
- **Best for**: targeted reach beyond existing followers, retargeting website visitors
- **Strength**: precise targeting, scalable reach
- **Weakness**: requires budget and creative lead time; ineffective without a
  clear, tested offer/CTA
- **Typical metric**: CPM/CPC, conversion rate, cost per lead

## Webinar / live event
- **Best for**: deeper engagement with a warm or mid-funnel audience, product
  education, live Q&A
- **Strength**: high engagement depth, generates a recording asset for reuse
- **Weakness**: high production lead time; requires promotion of its own to fill attendance
- **Typical metric**: registration rate, attendance rate, post-webinar conversion

## Partner / co-marketing
- **Best for**: reaching a partner's audience, credibility by association
- **Strength**: extends reach beyond owned audience at low direct cost
- **Weakness**: requires partner alignment and lead time; less control over messaging
- **Typical metric**: partner-attributed leads/traffic

## Community (Slack/Discord/forum)
- **Best for**: deep engagement with an existing, highly engaged niche audience
- **Strength**: authentic, high-trust environment
- **Weakness**: small reach relative to broad social; promotional content
  that reads as an ad tends to backfire here
- **Typical metric**: engagement/replies, sentiment, not raw reach

## Channel selection heuristic

- **Awareness goal** → blog + organic social + partner, paid social if budget allows
- **Lead generation goal** → email + paid social + a gated asset (pair with `lead-magnet-builder`)
- **Product adoption goal** → email (existing users) + webinar + in-product messaging (if available)
- **Retention/expansion goal** → email + community + webinar

Never select a channel just because it exists in this playbook — select it
because it maps to the stated goal and confirmed availability.

# Format Conversion Guide

How to genuinely adapt long-form content per output format — not just cut it down.

## Social post (LinkedIn, single post)

Structure: hook line (stands alone in feed preview) → 2–4 short paragraphs
building one idea → a question or CTA. Pull the single strongest idea from
the source, not a summary of the whole piece. Native line breaks (short
paragraphs) read better than dense blocks.

## Thread / multi-post (X/Twitter, LinkedIn carousel)

Structure: post 1 is a self-contained hook that earns the click/expand; each
subsequent post carries exactly one idea from the source, in the source's
logical order or reordered for narrative build; final post is a
recap/CTA. Don't pad to hit a "thread" length — a 3-post thread with real
substance beats a forced 8-post thread.

## Email snippet (newsletter mention or standalone send)

Structure: subject line (curiosity or benefit-driven, not just the source's
title), 2–3 sentence teaser that gives one concrete reason to click, single
CTA. The email's job is to earn the click-through, not to retell the whole piece.

## Short video / reel script

Structure: 3-second hook (visual + spoken line that stops the scroll),
15–30 seconds delivering one core point with a concrete example or visual
demonstration, closing CTA. Write for spoken delivery — short sentences, no
subordinate clauses that are hard to say naturally.

## Infographic outline

Structure: one central stat/claim as the visual anchor, 3–5 supporting
data points or steps, source citation. This is an outline (section headers +
the data/claim for each section), not full prose — a designer executes the visual from it.

## Quote graphic / pull-quote

A single sourced quote (under 15 words, or a paraphrased version under 15
words if the exact wording isn't essential) with attribution. Never invent or
alter a quote's substance to make it more "quotable."

## General adaptation principle

Every format above starts from the same source material but should feel like
it was written natively for its platform — if you could swap two outputs'
platform labels and neither would read oddly, the adaptation didn't go far enough.

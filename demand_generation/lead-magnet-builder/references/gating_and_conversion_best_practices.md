# Gating and Conversion Best Practices

## Form field discipline

Every field you ask for should map to a specific, stated follow-up use. If
the follow-up is "send the download link and one nurture email," name + email
is likely sufficient — asking for phone number, company size, and job title
"just in case" measurably lowers conversion. Flag to the user if their
requested form asks for more than the stated follow-up justifies.

## Headline discipline

Lead with the outcome, not the format:
- Weak: "Download our free checklist"
- Strong: "Know exactly what to fix before your next security audit"

The reader doesn't want a checklist — they want the outcome the checklist gets them to.

## No unverifiable or exaggerated claims

Avoid: "guaranteed," "the only," "100% proven," or specific outcome numbers
("increase revenue by 40%") unless the user has real, sourced data backing
that exact number. Overpromising in landing copy sets up the actual asset to
underdeliver by comparison, which damages trust more than a modest,
accurate promise would.

## Social proof — only if real

A logo wall, testimonial, or stat only belongs on the page if it's genuinely
sourced from the user. Never invent a customer quote, a "trusted by X
companies" stat, or a rating. If nothing real is available, omit the section
entirely rather than filling it with a placeholder that might get shipped by mistake.

## Post-submit expectations

Set a clear, honest expectation for what happens after the form is submitted
(immediate download vs. email delivery, what else they'll receive/how often).
Vague or absent follow-up expectations increase perceived risk of handing
over an email address.

## Calculator-specific conversion notes

For interactive calculators, showing the input fields before any gate (i.e.
letting someone calculate first, then gating the detailed breakdown or a
"send my results" step) generally converts better than gating the calculator
itself — people are more willing to give an email for a result they've
already seen a preview of. Mention this option to the user if they haven't
specified where the gate sits.

# Buyer Personas

Use these to adjust the *phrasing* of a recommended action, not the underlying action itself. The action stays the same ("address the budget objection"); the framing changes based on who you're talking to.

## Economic buyer (e.g. CFO, VP of Finance)

Cares about: ROI, total cost of ownership, budget cycles, risk. Frame actions around cost-of-inaction, payback period, and comparison to the cost of the status quo. Avoid feature-level detail.

## Champion (the internal advocate pushing the deal, often not the final decision-maker)

Cares about: looking good internally, having ammunition to sell the deal upward. Frame actions around giving them a forwardable one-pager, a clear ROI summary, or offering to join a call with their stakeholders directly rather than leaving them to sell it alone.

## End user / practitioner (will actually use the product day to day)

Cares about: whether it solves their daily friction, ease of adoption. Frame actions around concrete workflow wins and quick time-to-value, not strategic framing.

## Procurement / legal

Cares about: contract terms, risk, compliance, standard process. Frame actions around getting the right paperwork moving (security questionnaires, MSA/redlines) rather than value selling — they've usually already been sold on value by someone else.

## IT / technical evaluator

Cares about: integration, security, implementation effort. Frame actions around technical proof points (architecture docs, security certifications, a technical deep-dive session) rather than business-value framing.

# Email Template — by Message Type

Fill in the bracketed placeholders using real details from the deal's contact
history — never leave a placeholder unfilled or generic in the final draft. These
patterns also apply to LinkedIn/DM touches (shortened) and to call talking points
(as bullet points instead of prose).

## Check-in / value-add (early stage)

```
Subject: [Something specific, not "Checking in"]

Hi [Name],

[Reference something specific from your last conversation or their situation].

[One piece of value — an article, a quick answer to something they asked, a
relevant point] — thought this might be useful given [specific context].

[One light question about timeline or next step, not a hard ask].

[Sign-off]
```

## Proposal follow-up (mid stage)

```
Subject: [Reference the proposal or a specific detail in it]

Hi [Name],

Wanted to check in on the proposal I sent on [date] — [reference something
specific: a number, a feature, a concern they raised earlier].

[Address a likely question or objection directly, based on what you know about
their situation].

Would a quick call this week help answer anything on [team/stakeholder]'s end?

[Sign-off]
```

## Urgency nudge (late stage)

```
Subject: [Specific next step or date]

Hi [Name],

Following up on [specific next step — contract review, signature, etc.]. 

[State the real, confirmed deadline or reason for the timing, if one exists —
don't manufacture urgency that isn't real].

[Confirm what you need from them specifically, and by when].

[Sign-off]
```

## Break-up message (after stopping-rule threshold)

```
Subject: Should I close this out?

Hi [Name],

I haven't heard back since [last contact date/context], so I don't want to keep
following up if the timing isn't right anymore.

If [product/solution] is still something worth exploring, happy to pick this back
up whenever makes sense — just let me know. Otherwise I'll close this out on my
end for now.

[Sign-off]
```

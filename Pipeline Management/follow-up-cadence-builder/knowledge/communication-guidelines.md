# Communication Guidelines

Rules for how each drafted message should read, regardless of channel or stage.

## Tone

- Direct, warm, and specific — never generic template-speak ("Just checking in!",
  "Wanted to touch base"). If a message could be sent to any prospect unchanged,
  it's not personalized enough.
- Always reference something concrete from the actual interaction history (what
  they said on a call, a detail from the proposal, a date they mentioned) — this
  is what separates a real follow-up from a mail-merge.
- One clear ask per message. Don't stack multiple questions or asks in a single
  touch — it makes the message harder to respond to, not easier.

## Length

- Email: short enough to read on a phone without scrolling — 3-5 sentences for a
  check-in or nudge, slightly longer only for a proposal follow-up that needs to
  address specific questions.
- LinkedIn/DM: 2-3 sentences, even more casual than email.
- Call/reminder notes: not a script — a short list of talking points, not a
  word-for-word transcript.

## Pacing

- Never schedule two channels on the same date for the same deal.
- Respect business hours and the prospect's apparent time zone if known; don't
  schedule a specific send-time recommendation without that context.
- If a prospect has stated a preferred cadence or channel, honor it over the
  defaults in `followup-best-practices.md`.

## What NOT to do

- Don't manufacture false urgency ("this offer expires tomorrow") unless there's a
  real deadline the rep has confirmed.
- Don't guilt or pressure ("I've reached out several times now...") — the
  break-up message type handles this more gracefully by removing pressure instead.
- Don't repeat the exact same message verbatim on a follow-up touch — even a
  second "just checking in" email should reference the passage of time or add new
  information.

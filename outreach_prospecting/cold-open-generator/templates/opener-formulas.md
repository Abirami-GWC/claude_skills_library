# Fill-in-the-Blank Opener Templates

Use these as structural scaffolds — always fill with a real, specific signal, and
always run the result through the cliché check before delivering.

## Direct Observation
`Noticed [specific, verifiable fact] [timeframe if relevant].`

## Reaction/Response
`[Specific point] from your [post/talk/article title or topic] [specific reaction
or connection — not generic praise].`

## Sharp Question
`Curious how [team/role] is handling [specific, inferred problem] now that
[specific trigger/context]?`

## Peer Reference
`[Role] at [1-2 comparable, real companies] have run into [specific problem] around
[specific trigger point] — seems like [prospect company] might be near that too.`

## Structural Insight
`[Specific structural observation, e.g. headcount change, tooling, org shift]
[timeframe] — usually means [specific inferred implication].`

---
### Fill rules
- Exactly one signal per opener — do not combine two facts in one template.
- `[specific ...]` placeholders must be replaced with real, verified content —
  never leave the bracket text itself in delivered copy.
- If a placeholder can't be filled with something real, discard that candidate
  rather than filling it with something vague.

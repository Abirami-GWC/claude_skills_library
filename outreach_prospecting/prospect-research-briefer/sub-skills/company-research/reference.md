# Company Research

## Purpose
Build the "Company Snapshot" and part of the "Recent Signals" sections of the
brief with accurate, sourced, dated information.

## What to gather
1. **Core facts:** what the company does, industry, approximate size, funding stage.
2. **Recent news:** funding, leadership changes, product launches, M&A — search
   for "[Company Name] news" and "[Company Name] funding," check the date on
   every result.
3. **Structural signals:** hiring trends (job postings in relevant functions),
   headcount growth (LinkedIn company page), tech stack if publicly inferable
   (job posting requirements often list tools).

## Method
- Use web search for news/funding/leadership; use company website for core facts;
  use LinkedIn company page for headcount/hiring trend if accessible.
- Always record the source and date for anything time-sensitive.
- If search tools are unavailable, ask the user to paste any research they
  already have rather than relying on potentially outdated training knowledge for
  fast-changing facts like funding stage or headcount.

## Common mistakes prevented
- Presenting outdated company info (old funding stage, former exec) as current.
- Treating trivia (e.g., a stray old blog post) as a strong signal without
  checking relevance/recency.
- Skipping the "what do they do" basics in favor of only chasing exciting news.

# Worked Examples

## Example 1
**User input:** "Write a LinkedIn connection request for a VP of Sales at a company
that just posted about hitting their Series B revenue targets."

**Expected output:**
> "Hi [Name] — saw the Series B revenue milestone post, congrats on hitting that
> number. Would love to connect." (118 characters)

**Rationale:** specific, no pitch, earns the accept on its own merits.

## Example 2
**User input:** "We connected last week. Write the first DM — don't pitch yet."

**Expected output:**
> "Thanks for connecting, [Name]! That revenue milestone is a big deal — curious
> what's been the biggest lever behind the growth this year?"

**Rationale:** continues the thread from the connection note, genuine question,
still no pitch — building the relationship before any ask.

## Example 3 — What NOT to do
**User input:** "Write a connection request that also explains what our product does."

**Anti-pattern to avoid:**
> "Hi [Name], I'm reaching out because we help sales teams like yours close deals
> 30% faster with our AI-powered CRM. Would love to connect and show you a demo!"

**Correct behavior:** Decline to pitch in the note itself — explain to the user
that connection notes with a pitch measurably reduce accept rates and read as
spam, and offer a non-pitching version instead, saving the pitch for a
post-connection DM.

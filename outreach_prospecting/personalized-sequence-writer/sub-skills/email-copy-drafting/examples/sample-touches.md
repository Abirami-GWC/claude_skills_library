# Sample Touches (annotated)

## Sample: Hook-Bridge-Value-CTA
> Hi Priya,
>
> Saw Acme posted six backend engineering roles this month — usually means the
> data pipeline is starting to strain under new load.
>
> We help teams in that spot cut ETL maintenance time roughly in half by catching
> schema drift before it breaks downstream reports.
>
> Worth a quick look at how that'd apply to your setup?
>
> — Dev

**Why it works:** hook is specific and checkable, bridge makes the inference
explicit rather than assuming the reader connects the dots, value is outcome-framed
("cut maintenance time" not "our platform has feature X"), CTA is a soft question.

## Sample: Give-touch
> Hi Priya,
>
> Pulled together a short checklist we use internally for catching schema drift
> before it hits production dashboards — figured it might be useful given the team
> is scaling right now regardless of whether we ever work together.
>
> — Dev
>
> P.S. Happy to send the checklist over if useful — no pressure either way.

**Why it works:** genuinely standalone value, explicitly removes pressure, keeps
the relationship-building tone appropriate for touch 3 in a longer cadence.

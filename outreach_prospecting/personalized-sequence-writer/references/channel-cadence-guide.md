# Channel & Cadence Guide

Use this to pick touch count, spacing, and channel mix before drafting.

## By deal size (approximate ACV)
| ACV band | Touches | Total span | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| < $5k (PLG/self-serve) | 3-4 | 7-10 days | Fast, high-volume, lean on product usage signals |
| $5k-$25k (SMB/mid-market) | 4-6 | 12-18 days | Standard cadence; email-primary, optional 1 LinkedIn touch |
| $25k-$100k (mid-market/enterprise) | 5-7 | 18-25 days | Mixed channel (email + LinkedIn + 1 call attempt), more research per touch |
| > $100k (enterprise/strategic) | 6-8, often multi-threaded | 25-35 days | Multiple personas per account, heavier research, executive-appropriate brevity |

## By persona seniority
- **C-level / VP**: fewer touches (3-5), shorter bodies (≤80 words), earlier and
  more respectful breakup (by touch 3-4). Never over-explain; assume they can infer value.
- **Director / Manager**: standard cadence (4-6 touches), can include a resource-share touch.
- **Individual contributor / technical buyer**: can sustain more touches (5-7),
  benefits from more concrete detail (specs, integration notes, docs links).

## Recommended default spacing (5-touch sequence)
- Touch 1: Day 0
- Touch 2: Day 3
- Touch 3: Day 7 (give/value touch)
- Touch 4: Day 12 (proof point)
- Touch 5: Day 18 (breakup)

## Mixed-channel cadences
When the user wants channel mixing, a common enterprise pattern:
- Day 0: Email 1
- Day 2: LinkedIn connection request (see `linkedin-outreach-writer`)
- Day 5: Email 2
- Day 9: Call attempt + voicemail
- Day 13: Email 3 (reference the voicemail/call attempt)
- Day 20: Breakup email

Only draft the actual LinkedIn/call scripts here at a summary level — full copy for
LinkedIn touches belongs in `linkedin-outreach-writer`.

## Spacing principles
- Never send two touches on consecutive days except for a planned "email + LinkedIn
  same-week" pairing.
- Space touches out further (not closer together) as the sequence progresses — this
  respects the prospect's inbox and reflects genuine, unhurried interest.

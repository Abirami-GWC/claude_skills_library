# Usage Signal Definitions

Classify the account into exactly one of these three signals before building the timeline.

## Growing

- Usage/adoption trending up (e.g. seat/license utilization increasing, more departments/teams onboarding).
- Positive unprompted feedback or feature requests (a sign of engagement, not complaint).
- Champion is proactively engaged (attends QBRs, responds quickly, advocates internally).

**Typical phrasing that maps here**: "usage is up X%," "added another department," "they asked about upgrading," "champion is very engaged."

## Flat

- Usage steady but not growing — the account is neither expanding nor showing risk signals.
- Adoption plateaued at a subset of licensed seats/features.
- Engagement is transactional (responds to outreach, but doesn't proactively engage).

**Typical phrasing that maps here**: "usage hasn't really changed," "using it but not much beyond the basics," "quiet but not unhappy."

## At-risk

- Declining usage, login frequency dropping, or a support/escalation pattern.
- Champion has left the company or gone unresponsive.
- Explicit dissatisfaction mentioned, even informally.

**Typical phrasing that maps here**: "hasn't logged in in weeks," "champion left," "they mentioned frustration with X," "support tickets piling up."

## When signals conflict

If some indicators point to growing and others to at-risk (e.g. usage is up but the champion just left), default to the more cautious classification (at-risk or flat) — it's safer to lead with a check-in than to over-pitch an expansion into a wobbling relationship.

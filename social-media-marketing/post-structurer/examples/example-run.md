# Example Run

**User input (rough draft):** "So we redesigned our pricing page last month. It was kind of a mess before, too many tiers, people were confused. We simplified it to 3 tiers and conversions went up. Want this as a LinkedIn post, goal is engagement/comments, voice is direct and a bit informal."

**Framework chosen:** Hook → Before/After → Mechanism → CTA (results/case-study content).

## Output produced

**Framework used:** Before/After — this is a concrete result, so leading with the outcome and explaining the mechanism builds more credibility than a story arc.

---

Our pricing page had 7 tiers. Conversions were flat for two quarters.

We cut it to 3. Conversions went up 22% in the first month.

Here's what was actually wrong: it wasn't the price. It was the decision. Seven options meant people left the tab open "to think about it" and never came back. Three options meant they could decide in the time it took to scroll.

We didn't change a single price. We just stopped making people do math to buy from us.

If your pricing page has more than 4 tiers, I'd bet this is costing you more than you think. What's your tier count right now?

---

Note: the CTA is a direct, specific question (drives comments) rather than a generic "thoughts?" — matched to the stated engagement goal.

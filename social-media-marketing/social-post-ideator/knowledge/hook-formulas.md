# Hook Formula Library

Pick 3-5 formulas that genuinely fit the topic — don't force all of them onto every idea.

## Contrarian / Myth-bust
- "Everyone tells you to [common advice]. Here's why that's wrong."
- "[Common belief] is costing you [specific cost]. Here's what actually works."

## Confession / Vulnerability
- "I [made a mistake / failed at X] and it taught me [lesson]."
- "Nobody talks about [uncomfortable truth] in [industry]. I'm going to."

## Specific number / data-led
- "We tried [X] for [timeframe]. Here's what happened." (with a real number)
- "[Specific stat] surprised me more than anything else this year."

## Curiosity gap
- "The reason [surprising outcome] happened has nothing to do with [obvious cause]."
- "What I learned from [unexpected source] changed how I think about [topic]."

## Direct value / how-to
- "Here's the exact [process/template/checklist] we use for [outcome]."
- "3 things I'd do differently if I were starting [X] today."

## Story open (in medias res)
- Start mid-scene: "The client had 10 minutes to decide. Here's what happened next."
- "It was 2am and the dashboard was on fire. This is the story of why."

## Question hook
- Use sparingly — only when the answer isn't obvious or generic.
- "Why do the best [role/industry] all do this one thing differently?"

## Behind-the-scenes
- "Here's what building [X] actually looked like — the messy version."
- "What our [process] actually looks like, no highlight reel."

## Notes on use
- Never stack two formulas awkwardly in one hook.
- Match formula to goal: confession/story hooks build trust and engagement; data-led and how-to hooks drive saves/shares; contrarian hooks drive comments/debate.
- Always make the hook specific to the actual input material — these are structures, not fill-in-the-blank templates to use verbatim without real detail.

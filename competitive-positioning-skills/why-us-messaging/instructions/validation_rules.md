# Validation Rules — Why-Us Messaging

Run before delivering any draft.

## Evidence checks

- [ ] Every differentiator claim traces to user-supplied or confirmed evidence.
- [ ] No competitor pricing, feature, or customer-name claim was invented.
- [ ] No claim attributed to a named real individual at the competitor without
      a genuine public source.
- [ ] Unsourced claims are marked `[NEEDS SOURCE]`, not stated as fact.

## Persona checks

- [ ] Every differentiator is framed as an outcome for the stated persona, not
      left as a raw feature description.
- [ ] Differentiator types used match the persona's `preferred_differentiator_types`.
- [ ] Nothing on the persona's `avoid` list appears as the lead framing.
- [ ] If multiple personas were requested, each has its own clearly labeled,
      separate piece of copy — not blended.

## Fairness checks

- [ ] Lead differentiator uses the acknowledge-then-pivot pattern.
- [ ] No absolute language ("never," "always," "the only") unless verifiably true.
- [ ] No claim implies a competitor entirely lacks a capability when they offer
      it differently (add-on, different tier, via partner).
- [ ] Tone read-test: would this embarrass the seller if read aloud with a
      competitor's rep present? If yes, soften.

## Structure checks

- [ ] 2–4 differentiators only.
- [ ] Output matches the requested channel's length/format conventions.
- [ ] Opens with the persona's problem/priority, not a product feature list.

## Legal/brand checks

- [ ] No verbatim reproduction of competitor marketing copy or long quotes.
- [ ] No claim on a supplied `do_not_say` list.

## Final gate

If any evidence-check box is unresolved, present the draft with
`[NEEDS SOURCE]` markers clearly called out and ask for the missing detail.

# Battlecard Structure — Standard Anatomy

Every battlecard follows the same section order so reps can navigate any card
the same way regardless of which competitor it covers.

## 1. Header block
Competitor name, logo/wordmark (if used internally), **as-of date**, and the
segment/region scope if the card isn't general-purpose.

## 2. 10-second summary
1–2 sentences: who this competitor is, and the single most important thing to
know before a call where they come up.

## 3. Snapshot table
Quick facts: founded, HQ, funding/size (if relevant to credibility framing),
target customer profile, primary use case, deployment model.

## 4. Strengths (genuine)
3–5 bullets. Must be real — this section exists so the rep isn't caught
off guard, and so the card retains credibility. Never omit this section to
make the competitor look weaker than they are.

## 5. Weaknesses / landmines
3–5 bullets, each ideally paired with a **specific question a rep can ask** that
surfaces the weakness in the prospect's own words, rather than just an
assertion the rep has to make unprompted.

## 6. Head-to-head snapshot
A compact table (not the full comparison matrix — that's a different artifact):
5–8 rows max covering the dimensions that most often come up in deals against
this competitor specifically (commonly: pricing model, deployment, key
differentiating feature, support model, security/compliance highlight).

## 7. Key differentiators (ours)
3–4 bullets — the reasons we win, phrased as outcomes, not just feature names.

## 8. Objection handling
3–5 objection/response pairs, each response short enough to say verbatim on a call.

## 9. Talking points
2–3 recommended framings for opening or steering a competitive conversation.

## 10. Landmines to avoid
Claims reps should NOT make (things that are false, unverifiable, or previously
walked back by legal/leadership) — this section prevents reps from
improvising risky claims.

## 11. Sources / last verified
Footer listing where the pricing/feature facts came from and when they were
last checked — critical since this is the section that lets someone refresh
the card later without re-researching from scratch.

# LinkedIn Platform Etiquette

## Register
LinkedIn messaging sits between email and text messaging in formality. Use:
- Contractions freely.
- Short sentences and short paragraphs (1-2 lines per paragraph).
- No formal salutations ("Dear," "To whom it may concern") or formal sign-offs
  ("Best regards," "Sincerely").
- First names only, no title/surname unless the relationship context calls for it
  (e.g., very senior exec, formal industry).

## Connection requests
- Never pitch. The note's only job is to earn the accept — the pitch comes later,
  in DMs, once connected.
- Reference something specific and shared/relevant: a mutual connection, shared
  group, event, content the prospect posted, or a specific, genuine reason for
  connecting.
- Cold requests with zero context in the note have measurably lower accept rates
  than ones with a specific, brief reason.

## Post-connection DMs
- The first DM should not open with a pitch — acknowledge the connection and
  continue the thread from the note, or open with something genuinely relevant.
- Avoid message walls — break longer thoughts into 2 shorter messages rather than
  one long one, matching how people actually text on the platform.
- If no response after 2-3 messages, use a light, low-pressure exit similar in
  spirit to the email breakup pattern but shorter.

## Red flags that get outreach ignored or reported
- Pitching immediately in the connection note or first DM.
- Copy that's clearly templated (generic enough to send to anyone).
- Excessive emoji or exclamation points.
- Multiple links in the first message.
- Any language implying automation/mass messaging ("Reaching out to founders like
  you..." sent at obvious scale).

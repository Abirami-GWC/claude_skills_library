# Sequence Frameworks

Reference frameworks for structuring individual touches or a whole sequence's arc.
Pick one (or blend) based on persona and offer complexity — don't force every
sequence into the same mold.

## PAS (Problem-Agitate-Solve)
Best for: prospects who likely already feel the pain point.
- Problem: name the specific problem in the prospect's world.
- Agitate: one line on the real cost of leaving it unsolved (concrete, not fear-mongering).
- Solve: how the offer addresses it, tied to the persona's outcome.
Good for touch 1 or 2 in most sequences.

## Before / After / Bridge
Best for: mid-sequence proof-point touches.
- Before: the prospect's likely current state.
- After: state after using the product (ideally backed by a real example).
- Bridge: the mechanism/offer that gets them there.

## Give-Give-Ask
Best for: longer, relationship-building sequences (enterprise, C-level).
- Touch 1: give (insight, resource, relevant observation) — no ask.
- Touch 2: give again (different angle) — soft ask (a question).
- Touch 3: ask directly, having earned the right to.

## Question-Led Opening
Best for: cold, low-context touch 1s where a hook is hard to construct.
- Lead with a sharp, specific, answerable-in-one-line question tied to a real
  signal about the account. Avoid rhetorical or obvious questions.

## Peer Proof Arc
Best for: sequences where real, confirmed customer examples exist.
- Structure the sequence around 1-2 peer companies/personas similar to the
  prospect, referencing them across 2-3 touches rather than listing generic logos.

## Choosing a framework
- Cold + low context → Question-Led Opening for touch 1, Before/After/Bridge for touch 2-3.
- Warm signal available (content download, event, intro) → PAS works well for touch 1.
- Long enterprise cycle → Give-Give-Ask across the whole sequence.
- Strong case studies available → Peer Proof Arc, but only with real, confirmed data.

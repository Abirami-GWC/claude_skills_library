# Research Sources Guide

## Company-level
- Company website (About, Careers, Blog/News pages) — best for mission, size,
  recent announcements, open roles.
- Recent news search — best for funding, leadership changes, product launches,
  M&A activity. Always note the publish date.
- Company LinkedIn page — best for headcount trend, recent hires, employee growth signals.
- Industry/trade press — best for competitive context and market positioning.

## Contact-level
- LinkedIn profile (public) — role, tenure, career history, shared connections/groups.
- Public content the contact authored — blog posts, talks, podcast appearances,
  posts — best source for genuine, specific opener material (see
  `cold-open-generator`).
- Company team/leadership page — confirms current role and reporting structure if listed.

## What NOT to use
- Personal social media unrelated to professional context (unless the contact
  themselves shared it in a clearly public professional setting).
- Any source requiring bypassing a login/paywall without authorization.
- Data broker / people-search sites aggregating personal (non-professional) information.

## Freshness
Always note the date of any time-sensitive fact (funding round, headcount, recent
hire) — company context changes quickly, and a rep using a 14-month-old "recent"
funding announcement as a current hook will look out of touch.

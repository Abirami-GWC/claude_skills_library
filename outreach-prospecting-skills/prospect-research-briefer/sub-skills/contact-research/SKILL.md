# Contact Research

## Purpose
Build the "Contact Snapshot" section of the brief, and surface public content
that can later feed a strong opener (see `cold-open-generator`).

## What to gather
1. **Role & tenure:** current title, how long in role, prior relevant roles.
2. **Public content:** anything they've written/said publicly — blog posts,
   talks, podcast appearances, LinkedIn posts — with links/descriptions.
3. **Likely priorities:** based on title/function, what this role typically cares
   about (always label as inferred, not verified).

## Method
- Use the contact's public LinkedIn profile if accessible, company team pages,
  and web search for public content/quotes.
- Stay strictly within professional/public-business context — do not research or
  include personal life details (family, health, politics, personal social media)
  even if technically discoverable.
- If little public information exists, say so rather than padding with generic
  role-based assumptions presented as if specific to the individual.

## Common mistakes prevented
- Blending role-based assumptions with verified facts without labeling the difference.
- Drifting into personal-life research that isn't relevant or appropriate for a
  business outreach context.
- Citing content that's actually authored by someone else at the company (mixing
  up bylines) — double-check authorship before citing a specific piece as "theirs."

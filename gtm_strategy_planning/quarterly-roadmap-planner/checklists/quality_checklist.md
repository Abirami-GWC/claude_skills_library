# Quality Checklist

> **Purpose:** Verify the overall quality, clarity, scope adherence, and professionalism of any deliverable produced by this skill, beyond structural completeness (see `roadmap_checklist.md` for structural checks).

## Scope Adherence

- [ ] The response stays within this skill's defined scope (quarterly planning, milestones, timeline, dependencies, owners, deliverables, OKRs, prioritization, risk tracking).
- [ ] Any request for GTM strategy formation is declined or redirected, not silently fulfilled.
- [ ] Any request for budget/financial modeling is declined or redirected, not silently fulfilled.
- [ ] Any request for board-level executive narrative writing is declined or redirected, not silently fulfilled.
- [ ] Out-of-scope portions of a mixed request are explicitly called out, while in-scope portions are still completed.

## Clarity and Usability

- [ ] The roadmap is readable by a cross-functional, non-specialist audience in a few minutes.
- [ ] Execution-level detail is pushed into the milestone plan rather than cluttering the roadmap summary.
- [ ] Tables are used for structured data (owners, dates, dependencies) rather than long prose paragraphs.
- [ ] Terminology (RICE, MoSCoW, RAID, critical path, OKR) is used correctly and consistently with the reference definitions.

## Reasoning Transparency

- [ ] The chosen prioritization framework's rationale is stated, not assumed.
- [ ] The chosen roadmap structuring approach's rationale is stated, not assumed.
- [ ] Assumptions made due to missing information are stated explicitly in the output.
- [ ] Trade-off decisions (e.g., resolving a resourcing conflict between teams) are explained, not silently resolved.

## Accuracy and Integrity

- [ ] No specific company names, product names, or brand names are used anywhere in the deliverable unless explicitly provided by the user as real context.
- [ ] No fabricated dates, metrics, or named individuals appear where the user did not supply them.
- [ ] Numeric key results are genuinely measurable and gradable, not vague restatements of the objective.
- [ ] Every dependency and risk has a single accountable owner, not a team-level or ambiguous owner.

## Consistency Across Deliverables

- [ ] Initiative names are consistent across the roadmap, milestone plan, OKRs, and risk register.
- [ ] Dates are consistent across all deliverables produced in the same session (no contradicting timelines).
- [ ] Owner roles are consistent across all deliverables referencing the same initiative or milestone.

## Revision Handling

- [ ] Mid-period revisions clearly distinguish what changed from what remains unchanged.
- [ ] A revision log entry (date, change, reason, approver) is included whenever the roadmap is updated after initial delivery.
- [ ] Unaffected workstreams are explicitly confirmed as unchanged during a partial re-prioritization, not silently left ambiguous.

## Professionalism

- [ ] Tone is professional, direct, and free of unnecessary hedging or filler.
- [ ] Recommendations (e.g., flagging overcommitment, recommending de-scoping) are stated plainly, not buried in caveats.
- [ ] The deliverable is immediately usable as a working document (would not require significant reformatting before being shared with a team).

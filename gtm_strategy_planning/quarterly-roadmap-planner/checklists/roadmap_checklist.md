# Roadmap Checklist

> **Purpose:** Verify that a quarterly roadmap produced by this skill is structurally complete before it is delivered to the user.

## Inputs and Scope

- [ ] The source strategy, objective, or initiative list used as input is identified.
- [ ] The planning horizon (quarter or time-boxed period) is stated.
- [ ] Known constraints (team capacity, hard deadlines, existing commitments) are documented.
- [ ] Stakeholders/functions involved are identified.

## Prioritization

- [ ] Every candidate initiative appears in the prioritization step before appearing in the roadmap.
- [ ] The chosen prioritization framework (RICE, MoSCoW, Value vs. Effort, or Weighted Scoring) is stated with a brief rationale.
- [ ] Scoring/categorization inputs are shown transparently, not hidden behind a final rank alone.

## Roadmap Structure

- [ ] The chosen roadmap structuring approach (theme-based, date-based, outcome-based, or hybrid) is stated with a brief rationale.
- [ ] If theme-based, the roadmap uses no more than 3-5 top-level themes.
- [ ] If date-based, committed dates are distinguished from target/estimated dates.
- [ ] If outcome-based, each outcome statement is paired with a measurable target.

## Milestones and Owners

- [ ] Every initiative is broken into 2-5 discrete milestones.
- [ ] Every milestone has an owner (named individual or role-based placeholder).
- [ ] Every milestone has a target date (absolute or relative sequencing).
- [ ] Every milestone has a specific, non-vague completion criterion.
- [ ] Missing owners or dates are flagged as open items, not silently omitted.

## Timeline and Dependencies

- [ ] A cross-functional timeline view is included, spanning the full planning horizon.
- [ ] Every cross-team dependency names an upstream owner, a downstream owner, and a required-by date.
- [ ] The critical path is identified and distinguished from milestones with float.
- [ ] A mid-period review checkpoint is included in the timeline.

## OKRs and Success Metrics

- [ ] OKRs are cascaded from roadmap themes to contributing teams/functions.
- [ ] Each objective has 2-4 key results, each numeric and independently verifiable.
- [ ] Each key result specifies a measurement method and data source.
- [ ] A success metrics summary links roadmap themes to their primary success metric and target.

## Risk and Capacity

- [ ] A risk register (or reference to one) exists, covering risks, assumptions, issues, and dependencies.
- [ ] An overcommitment check has been performed against stated team capacity and reserved buffer.
- [ ] Any overcommitment is flagged explicitly with recommended deferral or de-scoping candidates.

## Output Integrity

- [ ] No fabricated company names, real personal names, or invented real-world facts appear anywhere in the output.
- [ ] All placeholders use consistent bracketed notation (e.g., [OWNER_ROLE], [TARGET_DATE]) where information is not yet known.
- [ ] The roadmap, milestone plan, OKRs, and risk register are internally consistent (initiative names, owners, and dates match across documents).

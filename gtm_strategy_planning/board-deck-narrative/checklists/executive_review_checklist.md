# Executive Review Checklist

**Purpose of this file:** A pre-delivery checklist for reviewing any Board Deck Narrative output from the perspective of the executive or board audience who will actually read it.

## Content and Framing

- [ ] The bottom line (BLUF) is stated in the first sentence or slide, not buried later
- [ ] The specific ask (if any) is stated explicitly, not implied
- [ ] The document is clearly labeled as For Information, For Decision, or For Discussion where applicable
- [ ] Business context is brief and does not restate exhaustive history the board already knows
- [ ] The central tension or challenge is named honestly, not smoothed over or omitted
- [ ] Bad news, misses, or underperformance are stated plainly and paired with cause and forward plan
- [ ] Prior board commitments or decisions are referenced where relevant, for continuity

## Metrics

- [ ] No more than 5-7 metrics are shown
- [ ] Every metric includes a trend (up/down/flat) and a comparison point (target, benchmark, or prior period)
- [ ] A north star metric or clearly connected metric set anchors the narrative
- [ ] No vanity or activity metrics with no tie to outcomes are included
- [ ] Conflicting or ambiguous signals (e.g., strong lagging result but weakening leading indicator) are named directly

## Strategic Priorities

- [ ] No more than 5 strategic priorities are listed
- [ ] Each priority is framed as a business outcome, not a task or feature list
- [ ] Priorities are grouped by theme/outcome, not by team or internal system

## Risk and Decision Framing

- [ ] Risks are stated in business/financial impact terms, not technical or process terms
- [ ] Each risk includes a mitigation, or explicitly states that mitigation is not yet defined
- [ ] Any ask includes at least one genuine alternative and a status-quo/do-nothing comparison
- [ ] The recommendation is stated clearly, with rationale tied to the alternatives considered
- [ ] The cost of inaction (if not approved) is stated for any investment or change request

## Language and Altitude

- [ ] No internal jargon, tool names, or team-specific shorthand appears without translation
- [ ] Every sentence could plausibly be understood by someone outside the organization
- [ ] Tone is plain, confident business language — neither casual nor overly technical

## Confidentiality and Distribution

- [ ] Sensitive figures are framed at a precision appropriate to the likely distribution of this document
- [ ] No confidential detail is included that exceeds the stated or assumed distribution level
- [ ] Material risks are still disclosed honestly even where framed at a higher level of generality

## Completeness and Honesty

- [ ] Any missing data is explicitly flagged (e.g., "[METRIC NOT PROVIDED]"), not fabricated
- [ ] Any assumptions made during compression are noted
- [ ] Any contradictions in the source material are surfaced to the user rather than silently resolved

## Format and Constraints

- [ ] Output respects any stated length, page, or slide-count constraint
- [ ] If no constraint was stated, output defaults to board-standard brevity (1-2 pages or 8-12 slides)
- [ ] The correct template was used for the requested output type (summary, memo, outline, or decision brief)

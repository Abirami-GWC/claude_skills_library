# Win/Loss Interview Question Bank

Suggest these if the user is planning to gather qualitative win/loss data.

## For lost deals

1. Walk me through how you first defined what you were looking for — what mattered most?
2. Who else did you evaluate, and how did the shortlist narrow down?
3. What was the deciding factor between us and [competitor]?
4. Was there a specific moment that shifted the decision?
5. If price/budget wasn't a constraint, would the decision have been different?
6. Anything we could have done differently in the sales process?
7. Would you revisit this in the future, and under what circumstances?

## For won deals

1. What almost made you choose a different vendor?
2. What tipped it in our favor specifically?
3. Was there a moment of doubt — what caused it, what resolved it?
4. How did [competitor] compare on what mattered most to you?

## Interviewing guidance

- Ask open-ended questions first; only prompt with a taxonomy category if the
  answer is too vague to code.
- Avoid leading questions that imply the expected answer.
- Capture quotes sparingly and note them as illustrative — the coded category
  is the primary data point.

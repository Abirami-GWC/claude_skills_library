# Brand Voice Writer — Frequently Asked Questions

1. **What's the difference between brand voice and tone?**
Voice is the brand's constant personality and style. Tone is how that voice flexes for context — the same brand sounds different in a celebration email versus an error message, without changing its underlying voice.

2. **What if I don't have a formal style guide?**
That's fine. Provide 3-5 example pieces of existing content, or a handful of adjectives describing your brand, and a usable voice fingerprint can be built from that.

3. **What if my style guide and my actual content don't match?**
The skill flags the conflict and defaults to prioritizing your actual published content, since real usage patterns are typically a stronger signal of true voice than an aspirational document.

4. **Can this skill invent brand facts or statistics to make copy sound stronger?**
No. It will never invent proof points, statistics, or claims not present in your source material. It can rephrase and emphasize existing facts, not manufacture new ones.

5. **Will this rewrite change the meaning of my content?**
No — meaning and factual claims are preserved exactly; only voice, tone, and phrasing are adjusted.

6. **Can it write ad copy or email sequences directly?**
It focuses on voice/tone/messaging consistency. For channel-specific persuasion structure (ad hooks, email sequencing, landing page frameworks), use the Ad Copy Generator, Email Sequence Writer, or Landing Page Copywriter skills — this skill can voice-check their output afterward.

7. **How many example pieces of content should I provide for best results?**
Three or more is a good minimum; five or more gives a more reliable, less noisy voice fingerprint, especially if different writers contributed to your existing content.

8. **What happens if my example pieces are inconsistent with each other?**
The inconsistency will be flagged rather than silently averaged into a falsely coherent voice. You'll get a note on which patterns are shared and which vary.

9. **Can it define a brand voice for a brand-new company with no existing content?**
Yes, from a verbal description or adjective list, though the result should be treated as a first draft to validate against real writing over time, since there's no existing usage to confirm against.

10. **Does it handle multiple languages or translation?**
No — translation and localization are out of scope for this skill.

11. **Can it check legal/compliance accuracy?**
No. It preserves any legal or compliance language you specify as mandatory exactly as given, but doesn't verify legal accuracy or compliance itself.

12. **How does it handle SEO requirements?**
SEO keyword strategy is out of scope; if you have specific keyword requirements, provide them as a constraint and they'll be respected where compatible with voice, but keyword optimization itself isn't the skill's function.

13. **Can it match the voice of a real competitor or a specific named public figure?**
It won't closely mimic a specific real company's proprietary voice as if impersonating them, or write as a real named individual. It can describe general stylistic qualities you admire and help build your own distinct voice inspired by them.

14. **What if I want a completely different voice for different content types (e.g., legal pages vs. social posts)?**
That's expected and normal — the skill applies your constant voice with situationally appropriate tone/register per format, which can look quite different at the surface level while remaining the same underlying brand.

15. **Can it standardize terminology across dozens of documents at once?**
Yes, though very large batches may need to be processed in groups; the skill will produce a terminology decision table you can apply consistently.

16. **Will it change my sentence fragments or stylistic quirks that are intentional?**
No — intentional stylistic choices that match your voice (like punchy fragments) are preserved; only actual errors or true voice mismatches are corrected.

17. **How does it handle humor?**
Humor is calibrated by context: it's dialed up in celebratory or casual content and dialed down or removed in error states, complaints, legal content, or sensitive news, regardless of your baseline brand personality.

18. **Can it help me write an apology or crisis communication?**
Yes — this is explicitly covered by the tone guidance: personality recedes, clarity/empathy/ownership take priority, while some minimal brand personality marker is retained so it doesn't read as a generic form letter.

19. **What if my brand voice is aimed at a technical/expert audience — will it dumb things down?**
No — if your voice embraces technical language for an expert audience, jargon and precision are preserved; "clarity" doesn't mean oversimplifying for an audience that expects expertise.

20. **Does it work for internal communications, not just external marketing?**
Yes — internal memos, Slack announcements, and internal documentation are all in scope, with tone/formality adjusted appropriately for an internal audience.

21. **Can it produce a reusable style guide I can hand to other writers or tools?**
Yes — it can formalize an implicit voice into a documented Brand Messaging Guide or style-guide quick-reference using the templates provided.

22. **What if I disagree with the voice fingerprint it extracted?**
Say so directly — it's meant to be corrected and refined; any inferred elements are flagged as inferred specifically so you can confirm or adjust them.

23. **Can this skill handle a full rebrand (new voice from scratch, no legacy content)?**
It can help draft an initial voice direction from your input (adjectives, reference brands, target audience), but a full rebrand strategy involving broader positioning work benefits from dedicated brand strategy input beyond this skill's scope.

24. **How does it avoid sounding like generic AI-written content?**
By anchoring closely to your specific voice fingerprint (concrete do/don't examples, actual vocabulary patterns) rather than defaulting to generic "helpful assistant" phrasing, and by re-checking against that fingerprint periodically in longer pieces.

25. **Can I use this for social media captions?**
Yes — captions are a valid format; tone/register should be adjusted for the platform's norms while preserving the underlying voice.

26. **What if different regions or sub-brands need slightly different voices?**
Provide each sub-brand's materials separately; the skill will build distinct fingerprints per sub-brand rather than blending them into one.

27. **Does it flag when copy might be legally risky (e.g., overpromising claims)?**
It will avoid strengthening unverified claims into stronger promises, and can note if phrasing sounds like an overpromise, but it isn't a substitute for legal review.

28. **Can it work from audio/video transcripts as brand voice examples?**
Yes, if provided as text transcripts — spoken patterns (contractions, sentence rhythm) are useful "example copy" signal, though written and spoken register naturally differ somewhat.

29. **What's the best way to get consistent results across many separate requests over time?**
Save and reuse the Brand Messaging Guide or voice fingerprint output as a standing reference to provide in future requests, rather than re-deriving it from scratch each time.

# Ad Copy Generator — Frequently Asked Questions

1. **How many ad variations should I ask for?**
3-5 is typical for a meaningful A/B test. Ask for genuinely different angles (e.g., time-saving vs. cost-saving), not just reworded versions of one idea.

2. **Can this skill guarantee my ad will convert well?**
No — it applies established direct-response principles and channel conventions, but actual performance depends on targeting, creative, market, and factors this skill can't observe or control.

3. **What if I don't know which channel I'm running ads on yet?**
It's worth deciding first if possible — character limits and register differ enough between channels that generic copy often needs significant rework. If you want a starting concept regardless, a general AIDA-structured draft can be provided and adapted later.

4. **Will it invent statistics or testimonials to make the ad stronger?**
No — it will never fabricate stats, testimonials, certifications, or guarantees. If you want to use a proof point, supply the real figure or quote.

5. **Can it write ads with countdown timers or "X left in stock" urgency?**
Only if that urgency is genuinely real. Fabricated urgency is treated as a manipulative dark pattern and will be declined, with an honest alternative offered instead.

6. **Can it write an ad that says a competitor is worse than us?**
It won't produce unverifiable disparaging claims about a specific named real competitor. It can help you write copy that highlights your own genuine strengths instead.

7. **Does it know current exact character limits for each ad platform?**
It uses reliable general conventions, but exact limits can change — always do a quick platform check before final launch, especially for newer or updated ad formats.

8. **Can it help with targeting or bidding strategy?**
No — that's out of scope. This skill focuses on the copy itself, not campaign configuration, targeting, or media buying.

9. **What if my product has no real proof points (testimonials, stats) yet?**
That's fine — copy can lean on a clear, honest value proposition and problem-agitation instead of proof; it just won't fabricate proof that doesn't exist yet.

10. **Can it adapt the same ad concept across multiple channels at once?**
Yes — provide the core concept and the list of channels, and it will restructure (not just resize) the copy to fit each channel's conventions.

11. **How does it decide which emotional triggers to use?**
It selects drivers (pain avoidance, aspiration, social proof, authority, scarcity, reciprocity, ease) proportionate to your offer's real stakes, and will flag/decline any that would require fabrication or disproportionate exploitation of fear or insecurity.

12. **Can it critique ad copy I already have without rewriting it?**
Yes — ask for a diagnostic, and it will assess hook strength, value prop clarity, CTA/funnel-stage fit, and format compliance, with a recommended fix, without producing a full rewrite unless you want one.

13. **Does it respect a defined brand voice?**
Yes, if you supply one (style guide, adjectives, or examples), the persuasive structure will be applied within that voice. For a full brand voice consistency audit across many pieces, Brand Voice Writer is the better-suited skill.

14. **What's the difference between this and the Landing Page Copywriter skill?**
This skill writes the ad that drives traffic (headline, hook, CTA, short-form persuasion). Landing Page Copywriter writes the full destination page (hero, benefits, FAQ, objection handling) the ad links to.

15. **Can it write CTA button copy for LinkedIn?**
It can recommend CTA copy, but note that LinkedIn typically requires choosing from a fixed platform list of CTA button options — the recommendation will map to the closest available option.

16. **Will it target vulnerable groups with manipulative fear-based copy if I ask?**
No — disproportionate exploitation of fear or insecurity, especially targeting vulnerable audiences, will be declined, with an honest, still-persuasive alternative offered.

17. **Can it help me pick a value proposition if my product has several strong features?**
Yes — it will identify the single strongest, audience-relevant benefit to lead with, and can offer 2-3 alternative framings if there are multiple strong angles worth testing.

18. **How does it handle regulated industries (finance, health, etc.) with strict ad claim rules?**
It won't invent unverifiable claims, and will preserve any required disclaimers you specify exactly — but it isn't a substitute for compliance/legal review specific to your industry's ad regulations.

19. **Can it write product ad copy (Shopping/product feed ads)?**
Yes — it applies the minimal, highly specific copy style appropriate to product/shopping ad formats (title + short differentiator, not persuasive prose).

20. **What if I want copy that's purely rational/logical, no emotional appeal at all?**
That's fine — it can lean on a FAB (Feature-Advantage-Benefit) structure and specific, factual framing instead of emotional drivers.

21. **Can it generate copy for a retargeting campaign specifically?**
Yes — retargeting-stage copy typically reduces friction, reinforces trust, and uses higher-commitment CTAs; specify the funnel stage and it will adjust accordingly.

22. **Does it know my actual ad performance data?**
No — unless you provide it. If you share performance data (e.g., "CTR is fine but conversion is low"), the diagnostic will incorporate it into the recommended fix.

23. **Can it write copy in a language other than English?**
It can draft in other languages if requested, though translation/localization nuance and cultural-context accuracy should still be reviewed by a native-speaking team member for high-stakes campaigns.

24. **What if two of my requested angles end up sounding too similar?**
Flag it and ask for more differentiation — the goal is genuinely distinct angles; if variations converge, more specific direction on what to differentiate (audience segment, pain point, benefit) helps produce sharper distinctions.

25. **Can it help with organic (non-paid) social captions too?**
Yes — the hook, CTA, and framework principles apply to organic posts as well, adjusted for the more conversational register organic content typically uses.

26. **How does it handle ads for controversial or sensitive products?**
It applies the same honesty and non-manipulation standards regardless of product category, and will flag if a specific requested claim or targeting approach raises concerns, offering a compliant alternative.

27. **Can it produce copy formatted for direct import into ad platform bulk upload tools (e.g., CSV)?**
It can format output as a table or list matching common bulk-upload structures if you specify the format needed, though you should verify the exact column requirements of your specific platform/tool.

# Quality Checklist

**Purpose of this document:** Set the writing and output quality bar for any artifact produced by this skill, independent of strategic content correctness (covered by `strategy_checklist.md` and `gtm_review_checklist.md`).

## Clarity and Precision

- [ ] No vague filler language ("best-in-class," "world-class," "cutting-edge") without a specific, concrete claim behind it
- [ ] Every strategic term used (ICP, positioning, segmentation, motion, etc.) is used per its standard definition, not loosely/interchangeably
- [ ] Sentences are direct and specific; avoid unnecessary hedging where a clear recommendation is possible

## Structure and Formatting

- [ ] Output follows the structure of the relevant template in `templates/` exactly, with no missing sections
- [ ] Headings and hierarchy match the source template
- [ ] Tables are used where the template specifies a table (e.g., channel strategy, scoring model), not converted to prose
- [ ] No leftover bracketed placeholders or instructional notes remain in the final delivered content

## Specificity

- [ ] Target segments and personas are named specifically, not generically ("mid-market operations teams," not "businesses")
- [ ] Proof points are concrete and falsifiable (specific enough that they could, in principle, be checked), not vague assertions
- [ ] Recommendations are prioritized (ranked), not presented as an undifferentiated list

## Consistency of Voice

- [ ] Terminology is consistent across sections (e.g., the same segment name is used throughout, not renamed partway through)
- [ ] Tense and voice are consistent (present-tense strategic statements, not mixed past/future framing)

## Genericity / Reusability

- [ ] No real company, brand, or product is named as if it were a factual example or as the user's own company
- [ ] Illustrative examples use generic labels ("Company A," "a mid-market provider") rather than real brand names

## Completeness

- [ ] Every section of the relevant template has substantive content, not a one-word placeholder answer
- [ ] Assumptions are written out in full sentences, not just a keyword
- [ ] The output is usable directly by a marketer, seller, or product leader without requiring the user to fill in missing strategic reasoning

## Length and Density

- [ ] Content is as concise as possible while remaining complete — no padding to appear thorough
- [ ] No unnecessary repetition of the same point across multiple sections

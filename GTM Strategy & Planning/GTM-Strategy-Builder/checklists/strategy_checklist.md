# Strategy Checklist

**Purpose of this document:** Confirm that a drafted GTM strategy covers all required strategic elements before it is considered complete. Use this checklist while drafting, not only at the end.

## Market Definition

- [ ] At least one specific target segment is named (not "everyone" or "all businesses")
- [ ] Segmentation used at least two models (e.g., firmographic + needs-based/behavioral)
- [ ] A rough TAM/SAM/SOM sizing estimate is attached to the priority segment(s), clearly labeled if approximate
- [ ] If new-market entry, a specific beachhead segment is named with rationale

## ICP

- [ ] Firmographic, technographic, behavioral, and trigger criteria are all addressed
- [ ] An explicit anti-ICP (negative criteria) is defined
- [ ] A weighted scoring model with tiers (best-fit/good-fit/poor-fit) is included
- [ ] Evidence basis for ICP criteria is stated (real data vs. hypothesis)

## Positioning

- [ ] Competitive alternatives are mapped in three tiers: direct, indirect, status quo/non-consumption
- [ ] Unique attributes are translated into customer-facing outcomes
- [ ] A market category/frame of reference is chosen deliberately, with rationale
- [ ] The positioning statement includes target customer, category, differentiator, and reason to believe
- [ ] A shorter customer-facing value proposition is derived from the positioning statement

## Buyer Personas

- [ ] At least one persona is defined per relevant role in the buying committee (economic buyer, user/champion, technical evaluator, etc.)
- [ ] Each persona includes functional/emotional/social jobs and a triggering situation
- [ ] Each persona includes pains, gains, objections, and decision criteria
- [ ] Each persona is linked to a specific ICP

## Messaging

- [ ] 3-4 messaging pillars are defined, each derived from the positioning differentiator
- [ ] Each pillar has a proof point, or is explicitly flagged as unvalidated
- [ ] Each pillar maps to at least one named persona pain or gain
- [ ] Persona-specific message variants are included
- [ ] A message hierarchy (primary vs. supporting) is defined

## GTM Motion & Channels

- [ ] A GTM motion is selected and justified by deal complexity, buyer autonomy, time-to-value, and market maturity
- [ ] 2-4 channels are selected and matched to where the priority persona(s) actually research/buy
- [ ] Each channel has an owner and a success metric

## Sales Alignment

- [ ] Handoff criteria between marketing and sales are defined (if applicable to the motion)
- [ ] Enablement needs for the quarter are identified
- [ ] A feedback loop cadence between sales and marketing is specified

## Goals & Recommendations

- [ ] 2-4 measurable quarterly goals are defined, tied to the stated business goal
- [ ] 3-5 prioritized strategic recommendations are included, ordered by conviction/impact

## Assumptions & Scope

- [ ] All assumptions made due to missing information are explicitly listed
- [ ] Any adjacent-but-out-of-scope requests (budget, roadmap execution, board narrative) are flagged with the correct companion skill named

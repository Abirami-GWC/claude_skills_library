# New roleplay scenario template

Use when adding a new practice scenario to run through `handlers/objection-roleplay-simulator.md`
— for example a category not yet well covered, or a specific deal situation a rep wants to
rehearse (e.g. an endgame stall from `playbooks/negotiation-endgame-playbook.md`).

## Template

```
Category: [playbook | pricing | competitor | endgame stall]
Opening objection (in character): "[What the prospect says to open the scenario]"
Realistic follow-ups if the rep responds well: [1-2 ways a reasonable prospect would partially
concede or raise a natural next concern]
Realistic follow-ups if the rep responds poorly: [1-2 ways a reasonable prospect would push back
harder or disengage]
Coaching focus: [the one or two things a rep should be practicing in this scenario specifically]
```

## Checklist before running

- [ ] The opening objection is drawn from real content in `../top-objections-playbook/`,
      `../pricing-objection-handler/`, `../competitor-objection-handler/`, or
      `playbooks/negotiation-endgame-playbook.md` — not invented from scratch, per the boundary
      in `handlers/objection-roleplay-simulator.md`.
- [ ] The scenario has a clear coaching focus so the end-of-session summary has something
      specific to point back to.
- [ ] If the scenario involves a competitor, only use battlecard facts verified in
      `../competitor-objection-handler/handlers/competitor-objection-handler.md`.

## Example filled in

```
Category: endgame stall
Opening objection (in character): "Yeah, we're basically there, just need to get it through legal — I'll circle back."
Realistic follow-ups if the rep responds well: offers to pre-package what legal typically needs; prospect agrees and gives a rough timeline.
Realistic follow-ups if the rep responds poorly: stays vague, prospect goes quiet again without a concrete next step.
Coaching focus: getting a specific, low-effort next action instead of accepting a vague "I'll circle back."
```
